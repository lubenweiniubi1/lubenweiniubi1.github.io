## 1. Daily Stand-up

> 📖 中文导读：每日站会（Daily Stand-up）是你每天最重要的开口机会。通常不超过15分钟，每个人用1分钟左右说三件事：昨天做了什么、今天计划做什么、有没有阻碍（blocker）。本章帮你掌握站会高频词汇和地道表达。

### Core Vocabulary 核心词汇

| Word | Pronunciation | Meaning | Usage Tips |
|------|--------------|---------|-------------|
| deploy | /dɪˈplɔɪ/ | 部署 | "deploy to production" 部署到生产环境 |
| blocker | /ˈblɒkər/ | 阻塞、阻碍 | 比 "problem" 更专业，站会关键词 |
| progress | /ˈprəʊɡres/ | 进展 | "make progress on..." 在...上有进展 |
| wrap up | /ræp ʌp/ | 完成、收尾 | 比 "finish" 更口语化，站会高频 |
| look into | /lʊk ˈɪntuː/ | 调查、研究 | "I'm looking into..." 我正在调查... |
| priority | /praɪˈɒrəti/ | 优先级 | "top priority" 最高优先级 |
| follow up | /ˈfɒləʊ ʌp/ | 跟进 | "I'll follow up on that" 我会跟进那个 |
| stuck | /stʌk/ | 卡住了 | "I'm stuck on..." 我在...上卡住了 |
| pick up | /pɪk ʌp/ | 接手 | "I'll pick up the ticket" 我来接这个任务 |
| roll back | /rəʊl bæk/ | 回滚 | "We had to roll back the release" 我们不得不回滚发布 |

### Synonym Notes 同义词辨析

- **finish vs wrap up vs complete**
  - *finish* — 通用"完成"，任何场景可用
  - *wrap up* — 口语化，站会/日常对话更自然
  - *complete* — 正式，适合书面/邮件
- **problem vs blocker vs issue**
  - *problem* — 通用"问题"
  - *blocker* — 特指阻止你继续工作的障碍，站会关键词
  - *issue* — 偏技术语境，如 "performance issue"
- **work on vs look into vs tackle**
  - *work on* — 正在做（日常高频）
  - *look into* — 刚开始调查，还没深入
  - *tackle* — 主动去解决（带有积极性）

### Common Mistakes 常见错误

- ❌ "I finished the bug fix yesterday."
  ✅ "I wrapped up the bug fix yesterday." — 站会用 wrap up 更自然
- ❌ "I have a problem with the API."
  ✅ "I'm blocked by an API issue." — blocker 是站会的标准说法
- ❌ "Today I will do the refactoring."
  ✅ "Today I'm planning to work on the refactoring." — planning to / going to 比 will 更自然
- ❌ "No problem."
  ✅ "No blockers on my end." — 站会标准回答

### Sentence Patterns 高频句型

**Reporting what you did yesterday 说昨天做了什么：**
- Yesterday I worked on ___ and managed to ___.
- I wrapped up ___ and made progress on ___.
- I spent most of yesterday debugging ___.

**Sharing today's plan 说今天计划：**
- Today I'm planning to ___.
- I'll be focusing on ___ today.
- My main priority today is ___.

**Raising a blocker 说遇到阻碍：**
- I'm blocked by ___. Could someone help me with that?
- I'm stuck on ___ and could use some input.
- I ran into an issue with ___. Happy to discuss after stand-up.

**Quick status 快速状态：**
- Still working on ___. Should be done by EOD.
- ___ is on track. No blockers.
- Just a heads-up: ___ might take longer than expected.

### Templates 模板范文

**Normal day 普通一天：**
> Yesterday I wrapped up the login page refactor and started looking into a performance issue on the dashboard. Today I'm planning to finish the performance investigation and pick up a new ticket from the backlog. No blockers.

**Blocked 遇到阻碍：**
> Yesterday I was working on the payment flow but got blocked by a backend API that's not returning the right data. Today I'll follow up with the backend team on that. If it's not resolved by noon, I'll pick up something else from the backlog.

**All done 全部完成：**
> Yesterday I wrapped up everything on my plate — the cart page, the checkout flow, and the unit tests. Today I'm planning to do some code cleanup and help out with code review. No blockers, open to picking up new work.

### Practice 练习

**2. Translate to English 翻译成英文：**
- 今天我要重构用户列表组件。
- 昨天我完成了首页的性能优化。
- 我在这个 bug 上卡住了，需要帮助。

## 2. Code Review

> 📖 中文导读：Code Review 是日常协作的核心环节。你需要用英语给出有建设性的反馈，同时也要能清晰解释自己的代码。关键是语气——既要指出问题，又要保持尊重。本章教你 review 中的高频词汇、礼貌表达和常用模板。

### Core Vocabulary 核心词汇

| Word | Pronunciation | Meaning | Usage Tips |
|------|--------------|---------|-------------|
| refactor | /ˌriːˈfæktər/ | 重构 | "clean up" 更口语，refactor 更专业 |
| nitpick | /ˈnɪtpɪk/ | 小挑剔 | "Just a nitpick:" 表示小问题，不强制改 |
| concern | /kənˈsɜːn/ | 顾虑、担忧 | 比 "problem" 更委婉 |
| approach | /əˈprəʊtʃ/ | 方法、方案 | "another approach would be..." 建议替代方案 |
| alternative | /ɔːlˈtɜːnətɪv/ | 替代方案 | 常用 "an alternative is..." |
| edge case | /edʒ keɪs/ | 边界情况 | "Have we considered the edge case where...?" |
| trade-off | /ˈtreɪdɒf/ | 权衡 | "The trade-off here is..." |
| LGTM | /el dʒiː tiː em/ | Looks Good To Me | 审批通过的口语缩写 |
| ship it | /ʃɪp ɪt/ | 可以合并了 | 非正式，表示代码没问题可以上线 |
| address | /əˈdres/ | 处理、解决 | "I'll address this in a follow-up PR" |

### Synonym Notes 同义词辨析

- **concern vs problem vs issue**
  - *concern* — 担心、顾虑，最委婉，code review 首选
  - *problem* — 确定有问题，语气较强
  - *issue* — 中性，技术语境常用
- **suggest vs recommend vs insist**
  - *suggest* — 建议（最轻，对方可以不采纳）
  - *recommend* — 推荐（稍强，你认为这样更好）
  - *insist* — 坚持（很少用，除非是 blocking issue）
- **fix vs address vs handle**
  - *fix* — 修 bug
  - *address* — 处理（更正式，适用于 comment/feedback）
  - *handle* — 处理（偏实现层面）

### Common Mistakes 常见错误

- ❌ "This code is wrong. Change it."
  ✅ "I have a concern about this approach. What do you think about...?" — 保持礼貌和建设性
- ❌ "Why did you do this?"
  ✅ "Could you walk me through the reasoning behind this?" — 问思路而非质问
- ❌ "You should use X instead."
  ✅ "Have you considered using X? It might handle Y better." — 建议而非命令
- ❌ "OK, good."
  ✅ "LGTM! Just one small nitpick: ..." — 即使通过也要清晰

### Sentence Patterns 高频句型

**Giving constructive feedback 给建设性反馈：**
- I have one concern about ___. What do you think?
- Just a nitpick: could we rename ___ to ___?
- Have we considered the edge case where ___?
- This looks great overall! One small suggestion: ___.

**Explaining your code 解释你的代码：**
- The reason I went with this approach is ___.
- I considered ___, but decided against it because ___.
- The trade-off here is ___ vs ___.

**Disagreeing politely 礼貌地不同意：**
- I see your point, but I'm leaning toward ___ because ___.
- That's a fair concern. My thinking was ___.
- Good catch! Let me address that.

**Asking for clarification 请求澄清：**
- Could you elaborate on this part?
- I'm not sure I follow the logic here — could you walk me through it?
- What was the reasoning behind ___?

### Templates 模板范文

**Approval with minor nitpick 通过 + 小建议：**
> LGTM! The refactoring makes the logic much cleaner. Just one nitpick: on line 42, could we extract that magic number into a named constant? Not a blocker though — feel free to address it in a follow-up.

**Requesting changes 请求修改：**
> Thanks for working on this! I have one concern about the error handling in `processPayment()`. If the API returns a 500, the current code would crash. Could we add a try-catch block there? Happy to pair on it if needed.

**Asking questions 提问讨论：**
> Nice approach with the custom hook! I'm curious about the decision to use `useReducer` instead of `useState` here — was that for performance reasons, or for readability? No strong opinion either way, just want to understand the thinking.

### Practice 练习

**1. Rewrite to be more polite 把下面的句子改得更礼貌：**
- "This is wrong. Fix it."
- "Why did you write it like this?"

**2. Fill in the blank 填空：**
- Just a ___, but could we rename this variable? (小建议)
- Have we considered the ___ case where the array is empty? (边界)
- The ___ here is readability vs performance. (权衡)

**3. Translate to English 翻译成英文：**
- 我有一个小顾虑：这个函数太长了，能拆一下吗？
- 整体看起来很棒！只是建议把变量名改得更清晰一点。

<details>
<summary>📝 Answers 答案</summary>

**1. Rewrite:**
- "I have a small concern about this part — could we try a different approach?"
- "Could you walk me through the reasoning behind this implementation?"

**2. Fill in the blank:**
- nitpick
- edge
- trade-off

**3. Translate:**
- I have one small concern: this function is quite long. Could we break it up?
- Overall looks great! Just a suggestion — maybe use a clearer variable name.

</details>

## 3. Sprint Meetings

> 📖 中文导读：Sprint会议包括计划会（Planning）、每日站会（见Chapter 1）、评审会（Review）和回顾会（Retrospective）。本章重点覆盖需求讨论、工作量估算、以及回顾会上的英语表达。这些会议通常需要你表达观点、讨价还价、以及给出建设性反馈。

### Core Vocabulary 核心词汇

| Word | Pronunciation | Meaning | Usage Tips |
|------|--------------|---------|-------------|
| sprint | /sprɪnt/ | 迭代 | 通常1-2周为一个sprint |
| backlog | /ˈbæklɒɡ/ | 待办列表 | "I'll add it to the backlog" |
| story point | /ˈstɔːri pɔɪnt/ | 故事点 | 用于估算工作量，"How many story points is this?" |
| estimate | /ˈestɪmeɪt/ | 估算 | "My rough estimate is 5 points" |
| priority | /praɪˈɒrəti/ | 优先级 | "This is high priority" |
| scope | /skəʊp/ | 范围 | "Let's keep the scope small" |
| milestone | /ˈmaɪlstəʊn/ | 里程碑 | "The milestone is end of Q2" |
| retrospective | /ˌretrəˈspektɪv/ | 回顾会 | 常简称为 "retro" |
| action item | /ˈækʃən ˈaɪtəm/ | 行动项 | Retro 产出的待办事项 |
| capacity | /kəˈpæsəti/ | 产能 | "What's the team's capacity this sprint?" |
| spike | /spaɪk/ | 技术调研 | "Let's timebox a spike for this" |
| stretch goal | /stretʃ ɡəʊl/ | 挑战目标 | "This is a stretch goal, not a commitment" |

### Synonym Notes 同义词辨析

- **estimate vs guesstimate vs ballpark**
  - *estimate* — 正式估算
  - *guesstimate* — 猜测式估算（非正式，信息不足时用）
  - *ballpark* — 大致范围，"a ballpark figure" 大概数字
- **priority vs urgency vs importance**
  - *priority* — 优先级（综合考虑）
  - *urgency* — 紧急程度（时间维度）
  - *importance* — 重要程度（价值维度）

### Common Mistakes 常见错误

- ❌ "This task is very big."
  ✅ "This task is quite large in scope. Should we break it down?" — scope 是会议关键词
- ❌ "I think it needs 3 days."
  ✅ "I'd estimate this at about 5 story points." — 用 story points 而非天数
- ❌ "The meeting was useless."
  ✅ "I think we could make the meeting more effective by ___." — retro 中保持建设性

### Sentence Patterns 高频句型

**Estimating work 估算工作量：**
- I'd estimate this at ___ story points.
- This feels like a ___ to me. What does everyone else think?
- We might need a spike to figure out the effort on this one.

**Discussing requirements 讨论需求：**
- Could we clarify the acceptance criteria for ___?
- What's the expected behavior when ___?
- I think we should keep the scope small for the first iteration.

**Retro feedback 回顾会反馈：**
- One thing that went well this sprint was ___.
- I think we could improve ___ by ___.
- An action item from my side: ___.

### Templates 模板范文

**Sprint planning estimation 估点对话：**
> "For the user dashboard widget, I'd estimate 5 story points. It involves a new API endpoint, a chart component, and responsive design. There's some uncertainty around the chart library, so it could be a 3 or an 8 — maybe we should spike that first."

**Retro feedback format 回顾会反馈：**
> **Went well:** The PR review turnaround was much faster this sprint.
> **Could improve:** Sprint planning felt rushed. Maybe we could start grooming earlier.
> **Action item:** I'll create a backlog grooming checklist before next planning.

### Practice 练习

**1. Fill in the blank 填空：**
- I'd ___ this at 5 story points. (估算)
- Could we ___ down this task into smaller pieces? (拆分)
- This is a ___ goal — nice to have but not a commitment. (挑战目标)

**2. Translate to English 翻译成英文：**
- 这个需求的验收标准是什么？
- 我们这周的产能大概是多少？

**3. Write a short retro note 写一段简短的回顾反馈：**
写一件这次迭代做得好的事 + 一件可以改进的事。

## 4. Slack / Chat

> 📖 中文导读：Slack（或 Teams/Discord）是外企的"办公室"。你不会每个消息都完美，但有几个高频短语和格式掌握了，沟通效率会大大提高。本章覆盖常用的 chat 缩写、提问方式、以及不同场景的消息模板。

### Core Vocabulary 核心词汇

| Word | Pronunciation | Meaning | Usage Tips |
|------|--------------|---------|-------------|
| ping | /pɪŋ/ | 提醒/联系 | "I'll ping you when it's ready" |
| heads-up | /hedz ʌp/ | 提前告知 | "Just a heads-up: I'm deploying to staging" |
| FYI | /ef waɪ aɪ/ | For Your Information | 分享信息，不需要回复 |
| loop in | /luːp ɪn/ | 拉入群聊/告知 | "Let me loop in the design team" |
| follow up | /ˈfɒləʊ ʌp/ | 跟进 | "Following up on this — any updates?" |
| bump | /bʌmp/ | 顶一下消息 | "Bumping this thread in case it got buried" |
| thread | /θred/ | 消息线程 | "Let's take this to a thread" |
| DM | /diː em/ | Direct Message | "Feel free to DM me" |
| cc | /siː siː/ | 抄送 | "cc @manager for visibility" |
| EOD | /iː əʊ diː/ | End of Day | "I'll have it done by EOD" |
| EOW | /iː əʊ ˈdʌbəljuː/ | End of Week | "Targeting EOW for the release" |
| TL;DR | /tiː el diː ɑːr/ | Too Long; Didn't Read | 摘要，"TL;DR: the API is down" |

### Synonym Notes 同义词辨析

- **ping vs message vs reach out**
  - *ping* — 最随意，"I'll ping you" = 我回头联系你
  - *message* — 中性，就是发消息
  - *reach out* — 稍微正式，常用于第一次联系或跨团队沟通
- **heads-up vs FYI vs just so you know**
  - *heads-up* — 提醒，通常需要对方注意
  - *FYI* — 纯分享信息，不需要回复
  - *just so you know* — 口头版 FYI，更口语

### Common Mistakes 常见错误

- ❌ "Hey, can you help me?"
  ✅ "Hey, quick question about the login API — is it expecting a POST or PUT?" — 把问题直接说出来，别等对方回复再问
- ❌ "OK."
  ✅ "Got it, thanks!" / "Makes sense!" — 单字回复容易显得冷漠
- ❌ Sending only "Hi" and waiting
  ✅ "Hi! Quick question about ___" — 别只发"在吗"，直接把事说了（参考 nohello.net）

### Sentence Patterns 高频句型

**Asking questions 提问：**
- Quick question: ___?
- Does anyone know if ___?
- Not sure who owns this, but ___?

**Sharing updates 分享进展：**
- Quick update: ___ is now live on staging.
- Heads-up: I'm about to merge ___.
- FYI: the design spec for ___ is ready for review.

**Asking for help 请求帮助：**
- Could someone take a look at this PR when you have a moment?
- I'm running into an issue with ___. Anyone dealt with this before?
- Happy to jump on a quick call if that's easier.

**Following up 跟进：**
- Following up on this — any updates?
- Gentle bump on ___. No rush, just want to keep it on the radar.

### Templates 模板范文

**Asking a question 提问：**
> Hey team! Quick question: is the `/api/users` endpoint paginated by default, or do we need to pass `?page=1` explicitly? Looking at the integration right now and the docs aren't clear. Thanks!

**Sharing an update 分享更新：**
> Heads-up: just deployed the cart refactor to staging. Did a quick smoke test and everything looks good. @qa could you run through the checkout flow when you get a chance?

**Asking for help 求助：**
> Hey, I'm getting a weird CORS error when calling the payments API from localhost — `Access-Control-Allow-Origin` is set to `*` but the browser is still blocking it. Anyone seen this before? Here's the error: [screenshot]

### Practice 练习

**1. Rewrite to be more effective 改写消息使其更高效：**
- "Hello" (然后等回复)
- "Help me" (没有上下文)

**2. Fill in the blank 填空：**
- ___ update: the fix is now live on production. (简短)
- Let me ___ in the backend team on this thread. (拉入)
- ___ this message in case it got buried. (顶一下)

**3. Write a Slack message 写一条 Slack 消息：**
你需要问后端同事一个 API 返回格式的问题。写一条消息。

## 5. Pair Programming

> 📖 中文导读：结对编程（Pair Programming）是两个人共用一台电脑写代码——一人"驾驶"（Driver）写代码，一人"导航"（Navigator）观察和指导。这章帮你掌握结对时常用的口语表达，让你能自然地边写代码边用英语交流思路。

### Core Vocabulary 核心词汇

| Word | Pronunciation | Meaning | Usage Tips |
|------|--------------|---------|-------------|
| drive | /draɪv/ | 驾驶（写代码） | "You drive, I'll navigate" |
| navigate | /ˈnævɪɡeɪt/ | 导航（观察指导） | "Let me navigate for a bit" |
| debug | /ˌdiːˈbʌɡ/ | 调试 | "Let's debug this together" |
| breakpoint | /ˈbreɪkpɔɪnt/ | 断点 | "Let's set a breakpoint here" |
| console.log | /ˈkɒnsəʊl dɒt lɒɡ/ | 打印日志 | "Can you console.log this value?" |
| variable | /ˈveəriəbəl/ | 变量 | "What's this variable holding?" |
| logic | /ˈlɒdʒɪk/ | 逻辑 | "The logic here is a bit confusing" |
| flow | /fləʊ/ | 流程 | "Let me walk through the flow" |
| refactor | /ˌriːˈfæktər/ | 重构 | "Let's refactor this into a helper function" |
| edge case | /edʒ keɪs/ | 边界情况 | "What about the edge case where the array is empty?" |
| call stack | /kɔːl stæk/ | 调用栈 | "Let's check the call stack" |
| gotcha | /ˈɡɒtʃə/ | 陷阱/坑 | "There's a gotcha with this API" |

### Synonym Notes 同义词辨析

- **drive vs code vs type**
  - *drive* — 结对编程专用，"Do you want to drive?"
  - *code* — 泛称写代码
  - *type* — 打字（在结对语境中略显奇怪）
- **navigate vs guide vs review**

| **R**esult | 成果和影响 | "As a result...", "This led to a 30% improvement in...", "The outcome was..." |

### Synonym Notes 同义词辨析

- **regarding vs about vs concerning**
  - *regarding* — 关于，邮件中最常用，正式但不生硬
  - *about* — 关于，口语/日常 chat 更常用，邮件中略显随意
  - *concerning* — 关于/涉及，比 regarding 更正式，偏法律/合规语境
- **follow up vs check in vs circle back**
  - *follow up* — 跟进（最常用），表示继续推进之前的事项
  - *check in* — 询问进展，语气更轻，不带有催促感
  - *circle back* — 回头再议，表示暂时搁置但会回来讨论
- **touch base vs catch up vs connect**
  - *touch base* — 简短沟通，通常有具体事项要确认
  - *catch up* — 叙旧/聊聊近况，更偏向非正式的个人交流
  - *connect* — 建立联系，常用于初次接触或跨团队
- **Best regards vs Cheers vs Sincerely**
  - *Best regards* — 最通用的邮件结尾，适合任何场景
  - *Cheers* — 英式/澳式，轻松随意，适合日常内部邮件
  - *Sincerely* — 最正式，通常用于求职信或正式商务函件

### Sentence Patterns — Opening Lines 开场白

- Hope you're doing well.
- Hope this email finds you well. (更正式)
- Just following up on ___.
- I'm reaching out regarding ___.
- Thanks for your time on [date/call].
- Quick question: ___?

### Sentence Patterns — Making Requests 提出请求

- Could you please ___ when you get a chance?
- Would you be able to ___ by [date]?
- I was wondering if you could ___.
- When you have a moment, could you take a look at ___?

### Sentence Patterns — Closing Lines 结尾语

- Thanks in advance!
- Let me know if you have any questions.
- Looking forward to hearing from you.
- Happy to jump on a call if that's easier.
- Best regards, / Cheers, / Thanks,

### Sentence Patterns — Following Up 跟进

- Just checking in on ___ — any updates?
- Following up on this. No rush, just want to keep it moving.
- Gentle reminder about ___. Let me know if you need anything from my side.

### Common Mistakes 常见错误

- ❌ "Please reply me."
  ✅ "Please let me know." / "Looking forward to your reply." — reply 不加宾语时用 "reply to me"
- ❌ "I have a question."
  ✅ "I have a quick question regarding the login flow." — 直接说出关于什么
- ❌ "Thank you for your cooperation."
  ✅ "Thanks so much for your help!" — cooperation 太正式，日常邮件很少用
